The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel

My rating: 5 of 5 stars

Review

Enjoyed reading this book. In the first few chapters, I got a bit impatient with the extended narrative on simple things but the author introduced several interesting ways to think about money, investing, risk, happiness, and growth.

The idea of saving money just for the sake of it and not specifically for anything is anti-thesis of how saving money is being campaigned in the mainstream media.

The last section on how the united states turned from a savings economy (before world war II) to a consumer economy and how that is impacting the debt to income ratio metric is interesting.
